Metric Quick Explainer

Personal Activity Intelligence (PAI)

PAI is a scientifically backed metric that translates your heart rate data into a single, simple score. Staying above 100 PAI weekly is proven to significantly improve cardiovascular longevity and health outcomes.

VO2 Max (Aerobic Capacity)

VO2 Max indicates the maximum volume of oxygen you can consume per minute during intense exercise. It is the ultimate indicator of cardiorespiratory fitness and endurance capabilities.

Body Battery (Biocharge)

Uses Heart Rate Variability (HRV), sleep cycles, and activities to calculate your energy reserve score (0-100%). High sleep scores recharge it, while workouts and stress deplete it.

What do Sleep Stages Mean?

Deep Sleep

Crucial for physical recovery, cellular regeneration, tissue repair, and immune system strengthening. Typically comprises 15-25% of adult sleep.

Light Sleep

Facilitates mental relaxation and basic cognitive indexing. Prepares your body for deeper phases. Usually makes up 50-60% of a healthy night's sleep.

REM Sleep (Rapid Eye Movement)

Essential for memory consolidation, emotional processing, and creative synthesis. This is the primary dreaming stage, accounting for 20-25% of sleep.

Altimeter & Altitude Tracking Explained

Your Zepp watch uses an internal barometric pressure sensor (altimeter) paired with GPS data to calculate your current height above sea level. During outdoor runs, trail hikes, or cycles, it actively logs your maximum and minimum elevation to compute cumulative climbing meters. This allows you to track elevation gain and gauge the difficulty of hilly courses.

Zepp App Weather Integration

A common question is whether your watch records and stores weather logs. Here is how weather works on Amazfit/Zepp devices:

Phone-to-Watch Sync

The Zepp mobile app connects to third-party weather API services in the background using your phone's current GPS location coordinates.

Detailed Metric Explanation Guide

Understand PAI, Sleep Stages, VO2 Max, and Energy indices

🔋 Body Battery / Biocharge Energy Index

Your Body Battery (Charge) is an index from 0 to 100% that measures your body's energy level. It dynamically tracks:

  • HRV (Heart Rate Variability): When you are resting, the time interval between heartbeats fluctuates slightly. Higher fluctuation (high HRV) indicates high recovery and low stress, charging your battery.
  • Sleep: A long, restful night with deep sleep stages provides the maximum recharge percentage.
  • Activity: Physical exercise, heavy stress, and anxiety deplete the battery.

🏆 Personal Activity Intelligence (PAI)

PAI was developed by the Nobel Prize-winning HUNT Fitness Study. It uses heart rate, age, gender, and activity levels to calculate a score:

  • It is not based on steps; it is based on heart rate intensity. Running for 20 minutes with a high heart rate earns more PAI than walking for 2 hours with a low heart rate.
  • Your goal is to maintain a rolling PAI score of 100 or higher. Keeping a score of 100+ is clinically linked to a 25% reduction in cardiovascular disease mortality.

🫁 VO2 Max

VO2 Max is the abbreviation for "maximal oxygen consumption". It describes the maximum amount of oxygen your cells can take in and use during maximal exercise. It is highly valued as a metric because:

  • It tracks your physical fitness progression. As you perform cardio workouts regularly, your lungs become more efficient and your VO2 Max increases.
  • High VO2 Max levels are strongly correlated with cellular health, longevity, and high physical endurance.
